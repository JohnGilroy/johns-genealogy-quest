Family Background

Bankruptcy, bigamy, extramarital affairs, riots, forgery, imprisonment and deportation — alongside achievement, prosperity and artistic talent. Welcome to the extended family history of Val (Chris) and John Gilroy.

This site is a public companion to our private research database and WikiTree profiles. It brings together original records, newspaper reports, photographs, and family memories, shaped into readable biographies and themed “spotlights” for family and friends.

Some lives unfolded quietly; others were marked by scandal, hardship, or unexpected turns. Our aim is not gossip, but context — to understand people as they were, through the evidence they left behind.

From time to time we publish a short family history newsletter highlighting new discoveries, favourite stories, and recent additions to the site.

Our ancestry centres on four main ancestral lines:

Gilroy & Wilson

John’s paternal and maternal Scottish lines

Agricultural workers and servants from Haddingtonshire, Kincardinshire and South Lanarkshire who moved into Scotland’s central belt as farming and industry mechanised.

Reynolds

Val’s paternal line

Merchants, clergy and farmers from Yorkshire, Northumberland and Herefordshire, many later based in London, with lines traced back to King Edward III.

Law

Val’s maternal line

Agricultural labourers from Gloucestershire and Wiltshire, including family members transported to Australia after early 19th-century protests against agricultural mechanisation.

Living individuals are redacted on this public site. For our collaborative family tree (and to explore connections), please visit our family tree on WikiTree.
