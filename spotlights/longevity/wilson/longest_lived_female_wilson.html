Jane Lamb

1852–1943

Jane Lamb is the second great aunt of John Whitelaw Gilroy

In brief

The longest lived female from John's maternal line (Wilson), Jane Lamb, was the only known daughter of Alexander Lamb and Jane Cockburn. she was born on 17 April 1852 in Morebattle, Roxburghshire, Scotland.

She married James Penman Wilson on 21 Nov 1878 in Yetholm, Roxburghshire, Scotland and had 3 children.

She died on 31 December 1943 at Blackcastle, Crichton, Midlothian, Scotland, from myocarditis, general arterial sclerosis, senility, having lived for 91 years, 8 months, and 14 days.

Life and times

During Jane’s lifetime, much of Britain’s nineteenth century unfolded during the Victorian era, a period of industrial growth, expanding railways, and rapid urbanisation. The First World War profoundly affected British communities through enlistment, wartime industry, and local loss. The Spanish flu pandemic of 1918–1920 brought a deadly wave of influenza to British communities just as the First World War was ending.

Jane lived in Scotland, in the counties of Midlothian and Roxburghshire.

Jane worked as domestic servant (1878–1911). She was also employed as charwoman and school caretaker.
