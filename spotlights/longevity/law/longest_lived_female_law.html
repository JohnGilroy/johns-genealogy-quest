Fanny Miles

1864–1956

Fanny Miles is the great grandmother of Valerie Christine Reynolds

In brief

Female from Chris's maternal line (Law), Fanny Miles, was the twelfth daughter of George Miles and Esther Neat. One of 13 children, she was born on 9 June 1864 in Southrop, Gloucestershire, England.

She married James Thomas Law on 24 Sep 1887 in Southrop, Gloucestershire, England and had 11 children.

She died on 29 April 1956 at St Andrews Hospital, Chippenham, Wiltshire, England, from arterial schlerosis, having lived for 91 years, 10 months, and 20 days.

Life and times

During Fanny’s lifetime, much of Britain’s nineteenth century unfolded during the Victorian era, a period of industrial growth, expanding railways, and rapid urbanisation. The First World War profoundly affected British communities through enlistment, wartime industry, and local loss.
